Investment Account

An investment account contains securities such as certificates of deposit, bonds, stocks and treasury notes. An investment account has a linked bank account to manage the cash position when an investment is bought, sold or provides income. The investment account and the linked bank account may be managed as a single account by the financial institution or they may be separate accounts at the same or different financial institutions.

Each investment transaction has an associated action. You can specify an optional category to further classify the transaction. The remaining fields are used based on the action selected.

Account Functions

  • New Transaction – Create a new investment transaction.

  • Edit Transaction – Modify an existing investment transaction.

  • Delete Transaction – Delete an existing investment transaction.

Investment Actions

  • Buy – Buy shares of a security. Specify the number of shares in the Shares field, the price per share in the Price field, the commission in the Commission field and the cost in the Amount field. The share price times the number of shares represents the cost basis for the security. The amount specified will be deducted from the linked bank account.

    • The cost for a bond does not include accrued interest. Accrued interest is recorded as a separate Expense transaction for the security.

    • The share price for a bond is often quoted for a 100-share lot. For example, a $10,000 bond would be priced at $100/share for 100 shares.

    • It is convenient to set the share price to $1.00 for fixed-income instruments which reinvest interest. This makes it easier to record the reinvestment since the number of shares reinvested is the same as the amount of interest received.

  • Exchange – Exchange shares of one security for shares in another security. The Amount field is the cost basis of the new security. The Shares and New Shares fields record the number of shares exchanged for the original security and the number of shares received for the new security. The cost basis of the original security will be reduced based on the number of shares exchanged.

  • Expense – Record an expense for a security in the Amount field. For example, accrued interest paid when purchasing a bond or foreign tax paid when receiving a dividend. Use the Category field to classify the type of expense. The amount will be deducted from the linked bank account.

  • Income – Record income for a security in the Amount field. For example, interest for a bond or dividend for a stock. Use the Category field to classify the type of income. The amount will be added to the linked bank account.

  • Reinvest – Record reinvested income. The Shares field contains the number of shares received and the Amount field contains the amount of income received. The cost basis of the security will be increased by the amount of income received. The amount will be added to the linked bank account.

  • RtnCapital – Record a return of capital for a security in the Amount field. The cost basis for the security will be reduced by the amount returned. The amount will be added to the linked bank account.

  • Sell – Sell shares of a security. Specify the number of shares in the Shares field, the price per share in the Price field, the commission in the Commission field and the proceeds in the Amount field. The amount specified will be added to the linked bank account. In order to calculate the cost basis for the sale, the correct accounting method must be selected.

    • FIFO – First In, First Out. Shares are sold starting with the first shares purchased. The cost basis is the total cost of the shares sold. This is the normal method when selling stock.

    • LIFO – Last In, First Out. Shares are sold starting with the last shares purchased. The cost basis is the total cost of the shares sold.

    • Avg Cost – Average cost. The average cost of all shares is calculated and this value is then multiplied by the number of shares sold to get the cost basis. This is the normal method for a mutual fund. Once the average cost method is selected for a security, it must be used for all subsequent sales of that security in order to get the correct cost basis.

  • SpinOff – Shares of a new security are received as a result of a spin-off from the original security. The number of shares for the original security remains unchanged by the spin-off. The Amount field is the cost basis of the new security. The New Shares field records the number of shares received for the new security. The cost basis of the original security will be reduced by the cost basis of the new security.

  • Split – Record a stock split. The Shares field contains the number of shares received or lost. For example, in a 2:1 split of 100 shares, you would receive 100 shares for a total of 200 shares after the split (the Shares field would be 100). In a 1:2 split of 100 shares, you would lose 50 shares for a total of 50 shares after the split (the Shares field would be -50). The cost basis for the security is unchanged by the split.

Partial shares received from an exchange, spin-off or stock split are usually sold immediately by the broker and you receive cash in lieu of the shares. In this case, you should record all of the shares in the exchange, spin-off or stock split transaction. You should then create a separate sell transaction to record the cash in lieu. This causes the correct cost basis to be calculated for the remaining shares.
